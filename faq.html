FAQ

FAQ

Will I need a VISA to enter Morocco?

No, unless you are a Passport holder from South Africa you will need to apply for a visa before entering the country or wish to stay longer then a 3 month period.

If I stay with a family, will they accommodate my dietary requirements?

yes, the families will do their best provide food that you are happy to eat. In the case of anaphylaxis we must ensure you have all the right medical equipment/alerts with you at all times.

What kind of foods will they serve in the homes?

Main dishes are breads with egg, couscous, vegetables, chicken, lamb, tajine. Fruit is also common along with a few sweet treats and of course Moroccan tea.

How would I get from my accommodation to the work spot?

depending on the which type of accomodation you choice you can walk, catch the public buses or the taxi which we will explain to you on arrival. All very simple and easy to use.

Will there be people doing the same timeline as me?

During a busy period, yes. Off peak season may differ with the exact match ups, but there will be overlaps. If you’re worried about being alone then you shouldn’t be for a second. There is always someone at the working space whether it be a nomad like your self, one of us or a local writing a great novel.

Is this confined to certain times and meeting points?

No, not at all. This is your life changing trip done your way. If you want to just work one day and then the next join in and amongst the hustle and bustle all on your own then we’ll sit back and let you, giving you all the best tips of course.
